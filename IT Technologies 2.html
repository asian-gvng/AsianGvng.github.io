Autonomous Vehicles

What Does It Do?
Autonomous Vehicles can automatically drive for passengers without the need for a human driver. These are vehicles which interact with internal and external technologies such as other vehicles, Bluetooth, connected devices, navigation systems, automatons and other autonomous vehicles.
Cameras and sensors play a big role in ensuring the functionality and effectiveness of an autonomous vehicle. These cameras and sensors can detect and identify any given scenario as well as learn from new ones, as machine learning can be implemented into this technology.
Autonomous vehicles can also replace vehicles (in the future), or even replace the public transport system.
The public and the country will be able to save resources and money compared to renewing and maintaining a dated system.
They can be employed as taxis, or be privately owned for leisure and recreation. In the future we may encounter autonomous vehicles racing against one another.
Autonomous Vehicles can also be employed in optimising road safety or traffic design.

What is the Likely Impact?
This technology can help make the road safer for everyone. Traffic jams will become less and less common, navigation through busy and rough terrain are made easier, and people who drive to work daily will have time to rest inside the vehicle instead.
Gas emissions may lower as routes are optimised, and electric cars combined with this autonomous technology may have a great impact in cities with a large amount of pollution. The car manufacturing industry will also be challenged by this new technology, as the public leans towards sustainability and reduced gas emissions.

How will this Affect You?
Autonomous vehicles will have a great impact in a person’s professional and personal life. You will encounter less fatigued drivers on the road, due to being able to switch from manual to automatic driving, which may also mean less fatalities.
Pollution in heavily traffic congested cities will notice a decreased amount of pollution as vehicles work alongside one another, and electric cars become more and more common. Lastly, newer technologies, especially personal computers and devices may have more functions and needed tools when integrated into this technology. You may notice more phone use as users ride as passengers rather than drive.

Robots

What Does It Do?
Robots have a vast range of uses in our everyday life. The main purpose of robots are to make everyday interactions or tediums simpler and easier.
Robots can also ease physical labor or make monotonous work automated. You can also have robots become servers, providing different services for different needs or wants, such as having a robot butler or a robot cleaner.
Robots can be also used in emergency services, especially in areas where no humans can go, such as places such as Fukushima in Japan or Chernobyl in Russia.
In example, Honda’s ASIMO is a robot capable of human interaction and articulate movement. It can kick a football for articulate movement, and recognise human speech for communication.

Or another example: Boston Dynamics’ Atlas - which is an autonomous humanoid robot, capable of performing acrobatics and parkour, as well as long distance running, even Spot, which is a quad legged robot which imitates a dog but has an arm with hands and an opposable thumb.

What is the Likely Impact?
Robots can solve problems or create new problems involving robots.
An example of a likely impact involving robots, is the advancement of automatons. As machines learn how to learn, being able to differentiate different scenarios, or even be able to measure morality, will robots need to have protocols internally to ensure nothing bad happens?
Isaac Asimov’s book I, Robot - where Asimov talks about how robots must have an important and priority level protocol in which a robot must never harm a human being.


How Will this Affect You?
Your everyday life will be affected. This may appear as hard science fiction, but robots are a realized concept which do not have to be developed in a complex way. Robots can automate even the most simplest, easiest and the most tedious of tasks. They are made to make your life easier.
Robot-human interactions may create new ideas, new technologies for personal and professional use, and maybe improve human health and lifestyle.
Although over the years, people have expressed their concerns about robots taking over their jobs. This may be a reality that may someday be realized, but even if this does make, for example: factory workers obsolete - new fields will always be made as technology advances.
