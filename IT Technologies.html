Cybersecurity

What Does It Do?
Cybersecurity is a practice of protecting computer systems from malicious attacks which may hinder or maliciously control important systems such as systems found in hospitals, emergency services or communication. Cybersecurity also protects from theft of private data, damage to hardware, softwares, electronics and systems
Cybersecurity can also be offered in multiple forms, such as softwares like anti-viruses or protecting and preventing user-manipulation in the “Ring” layers which protect the lower levels of an operating system.
Cybersecurity in a few years from now may encounter newer kinds of attacks or vulnerabilities like Spectre and Meltdown, which were a major concern for computers, due to how lower levels of an operating system were compromised, security levels in which a user shouldn’t have access to.
Malicious software may also intercept software updates in the future, for example: Asus’s software update, which was hacked and a virus was injected into people’s computers due to a hidden backdoor access inside Asus’s servers.

What is the Likely Impact?
As computers become better, faster and stronger and as old and new jobs are becoming more technology centered, Cybersecurity is needed more than ever. The internet has always been a Wild West for information and the freedom it offers, it’s easier now to make a mistake and leak private data, and as more of the world becomes connected through the internet, the harder it becomes to maintain and renew computer systems which harness and process millions upon millions of information everyday.
Cybersecurity offers many solutions to problems already known and experienced in the IT world, but what separates Cybersecurity from your general IT job are the psychological aspects of understanding malicious attacks and a deep knowledge behind computers; offering solutions to problems not even addressed, or problems currently being brewed after some good information gathering. The likely impact is that you and I are vulnerable to cyber attacks, from client to server attacks to server to client attacks, Cybersecurity solves this by creating and maintaining a system of protective layers between your computer and a website’s server from unwanted interference.

How will this Affect You?
The effect of Cybersecurity in this ever-changing world will affect you and your family. Without your knowing your computer could already be infected with a malicious software currently logging and relaying your every keystroke as you type your bank credentials and private details into your computer.
And with hackers (hacktivists, script kiddies, and professional hackers) becoming smarter and faster in developing malicious software (malware) and all kinds of other viruses which can log information or control a computer remotely, people with little-to-no knowledge of securing their systems personally are vulnerable to even the most basic viruses. People nowadays use social media to browse photos, but how many people know about keyloggers being embedded into images? Or the fact public-wifi accesses are incredibly vulnerable to snooping of media, e-mails and general internet browsing?

Small Computing Devices

What Does It Do?
Small Computing Devices are personal computers (PC) that are small enough for portability and handheld use.
These devices work just like your everyday personal computer desktop or laptop; able to run small scale applications which require small amounts of memory, mostly less than 50 megabytes, to be stored in your mobile devices. The applications that run inside the mobile devices are developed to have user-fitted purposes, ranging from calculators, calendars, document editors/writers, management tools and fitness trackers, to games and multimedia players.
Small Computing Devices also have functions for text-messaging, phone calling and GPS navigation.

Small Computing Devices will have an important role in the future, especially in the classroom. As newer generations are introduced to technology, they will be learning about computer literacy. Small Computing Devices are great in teaching children how to operate them, they can be developed specifically for children to help with learning.
Small Computing Devices can also explore different fields, from business to farming. They can provide users the information they need in its portability. For example: Fonterra, in New Zealand, employs smartphones to monitor the humidity and nutrients of the grass within their farms, ensuring that their livestock are fed the optimum amount of nutrients.

What is the Likely Impact?
Small Computing Devices are everywhere. As processing chips become smaller and smaller, becoming the size of a fingernail, or as memory storage become small enough to fit in your pocket, small computing devices, too, will become smaller.
These can replace old technologies, such as your public transportation e-card, or even your credit card, or be developed into drones that are micronomical in size, you can even have an identification card inside the palm of your hand. Small computing devices can lead to newer technologies. This is evident in Arduino products or Raspberry Pi products, or the new Samsung Galaxy Fold or Smart Watches.
Small Computing Devices can be implemented in many things, even everyday products such as your earphones, toys and tools.

How Will this Affect You?
As mobile applications are developed to be compatible with modern mobile devices, you will be encountering a new phone maybe every three to five years depending on demand. You may be issued a company mobile device for office-work or for communication for business. You may also need a new mobile device feature to make tediums easier or automated.
You may also need to purchase a new phone due to the early stages of development of a newly released device, which may have some flaws that you, the user, will be disappointed about.
There are many reasons as to why this will affect you and everyone around you. The security of an application or the mobile device operating system may in time be vulnerable as mobile devices become better and faster, anti-virus software are currently available in the market, making it evident that as more information are stored inside a small computing device.
